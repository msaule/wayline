How it works

One route in. One finished itinerary out.

Wayline plans the route, the fuel cadence, the overnight hotel areas, and the Google Maps handoff as one system instead of leaving you to stitch them together yourself.

01

Find the route

Wayline uses Google Routes API to get the primary drive, total distance, duration, and route geometry.

02

Split the drive

Overnight areas are chosen by route progression and day balance, not raw distance alone.

03

Score fuel stops

Fuel stops are searched in a route window and ranked by brand preference, reviews, and detour distance.

04

Rank hotel options

Hotel picks focus on mainstream brands, ratings, and route convenience around the overnight area.

05

Return one itinerary

The result comes back as a readable trip plan with day links, notes, and exports.

06

Keep the key off the client

Live planning runs through backend routes, so the Google Maps key stays server-side.
